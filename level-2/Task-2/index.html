Sundar Pichai

A man with vision

Who is he and why should we know him?

Sundar Pichai is the current CEO of Google and Alphabet. Known as a successful innovator, he focuses on developing new products and services at Google.

Gaining Experience

In 1997, Sundar Pichai abandoned the Ph.D. program at Stanford University, accepting his master's degree in material sciences and engineering. He joined Allied Materials, a semiconductor manufacturer in Silicon Valley, as a product manager before leaving to pursue an MBA at the University of Pennsylvania. After graduation in 2002, Pichai accepted a position as a management consultant at McKinsey & Co. Having roles of both product manager and consultant at two leading firms, which require product strategy, execution, and team building, prepared Sundar Pichai for his new position at Google in 2004.

Success at Google

Sundar Pichai's interview at Google coincidently coincided with the launch of Google Gmail. Unaware of the new product, Pichai admitted his ignorance during his interviews and is remembered as the candidate with "intellectual humility," which Laszlo Bock, Google’s former senior VP of people operations, called one of the top qualities he looked for in candidates at Google. Pichai was well-prepared but managed a dynamic situation with candor during a moment of uncertainty.1 On April 1, 2004, Sundar Pichai joined Google as its vice-president of product management and was tasked with the development of Google Toolbar and Google Chrome, which grew to become the world’s most popular internet browser. Pichai launched the Chrome OS and Chromebook in 2011, headed the Android operating system in 2013, and became the leader of all products and engineering at Google by 2014. Pichai oversaw all of Google’s products and platforms, including Search, Maps, Play, Android, Chrome, Gmail, and Google Workspace. Tech industry gossip placed Pichai in serious contention for CEO of Microsoft, a position that eventually went to Satya Nadella.3 When Google reorganized into Alphabet in August 2015, Sundar Pichai was selected as Google’s CEO. He joined the board of directors of Alphabet in July 2017. Under Pichai's leadership, Google invested in new opportunities such as Google Cloud and YouTube and continues to research advanced technologies, including machine learning and quantum computing.
